How to Reset an iPhone to Factory Settings Without an Access Code

The Ultimate Guide

1. Preparation and Precautions

Before proceeding, make sure you have the following:

  • A computer with iTunes installed: If you are using macOS Catalina or later, use Finder.
  • USB cable: To connect your iPhone to the computer.
  • Stable internet connection: To download any necessary software and firmware.

2. Entering Recovery Mode

To reset your iPhone without an access code, you need to put it into recovery mode. The steps vary slightly depending on the iPhone model:

iPhone 8 or later

  1. Connect your iPhone to the computer using a USB cable.
  2. Press and quickly release the Volume Up button.
  3. Press and quickly release the Volume Down button.
  4. Hold down the side button until the recovery mode screen appears (showing a computer icon and a cable).

iPhone 7 and 7 Plus

  1. Connect your iPhone to the computer using a USB cable.
  2. Press and hold both the Volume Down and side buttons simultaneously.
  3. Continue holding them until the recovery mode screen appears.

iPhone 6s and earlier

  1. Connect your iPhone to the computer using a USB cable.
  2. Press and hold both the Home and top (or side) buttons simultaneously.
  3. Continue holding them until the recovery mode screen appears.

3. Using iTunes or Finder

Once your iPhone is in recovery mode, iTunes or Finder should automatically detect it and show options to restore or update the device.

  1. Open iTunes (or Finder if using macOS Catalina or later).
  2. Locate your device in iTunes or Finder.
  3. You will see a message stating that there is a problem with your iPhone that requires it to be updated or restored.
  4. Select the "Restore" option.

4. Resetting Your iPhone

Resetting your iPhone will erase all data and settings, including the access code. Here are the detailed steps:

  1. Confirm the restore action in the prompt.
  2. iTunes/Finder will download the latest iOS firmware for your device.
  3. Once the firmware is downloaded, the restore process will start automatically.
  4. This process can take some time, so be patient and do not disconnect your iPhone during this period.

5. Setting Up Your iPhone

After the restore process is complete, your iPhone will restart and you will see the welcome screen.

  1. Follow the on-screen instructions to set up your iPhone.
  2. If you have a backup on iCloud or iTunes, you can choose to restore your data from the backup during setup.

Important Considerations

  • Data Loss: This process will erase all data on your iPhone. Make sure you have a backup if you want to restore your data afterward.
  • Apple ID and Activation Lock: If your device has Activation Lock enabled, you will need your Apple ID and password to set it up after the restore.
  • Legal Implications: Bypassing security measures on a device you do not own can have legal consequences.
